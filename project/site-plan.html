🌍 Site Name

Zimbabwe Wildlife Conservation Network

Why this name was selected: This name clearly communicates the website's focus on wildlife conservation specifically in Zimbabwe. It establishes the site as a hub for conservation information, connecting visitors with Zimbabwe's unique wildlife heritage and preservation efforts. The name is descriptive, memorable, and immediately conveys the site's purpose to potential visitors.

Domain (for reference): zimbabwe-wildlife-conservation.org

🎯 Site Purpose

The Zimbabwe Wildlife Conservation Network website serves as an educational and engagement hub for wildlife conservation in Zimbabwe. The site provides:

  • Information about Zimbabwe's national parks and wildlife reserves (Hwange, Mana Pools, Gonarezhou, Matobo Hills)
  • Profiles of endangered and native wildlife species (elephants, rhinos, lions, African wild dogs, painted wolves)
  • Conservation success stories and current challenges
  • Ways for visitors to get involved through donations, volunteer opportunities, and wildlife adoption programs
  • Integration of live weather data for major parks using OpenWeatherMap API
  • Interactive Google Maps showing park locations

❓ User Scenarios

Scenario 1: The Wildlife Enthusiast

User: Sarah, a 34-year-old wildlife photographer planning a trip to Zimbabwe.

Question: "Which national park in Zimbabwe offers the best opportunity to see elephants in their natural habitat, and what is the current weather like there?"

How the site answers: The site provides detailed park guides with elephant population information, best viewing seasons, and live weather data from the OpenWeatherMap API for each location.

Scenario 2: The Conservation Donor

User: Michael, a 45-year-old retired teacher who wants to support wildlife conservation but doesn't know where to start.

Question: "How can I donate to help protect rhinos from poaching in Zimbabwe, and how will my money be used?"

How the site answers: The site includes a "Get Involved" page with donation options, adoption programs, and transparency reports showing how funds are allocated to conservation projects.

Scenario 3: The Student Researcher

User: David, a 19-year-old university student researching African wild dog conservation.

Question: "What are the current population numbers for African wild dogs in Zimbabwe, and what conservation efforts are underway?"

How the site answers: The site provides species profiles with population data and conservation status.

Color Usage Summary:

  • Dark Green (#1B5E20): Main headers, footer background
  • Medium Green (#2E7D32): Navigation bar, section headings border
  • Light Green (#4CAF50): Hover effects, success indicators
  • Amber (#FF8F00): Call-to-action buttons, important highlights
  • Warm Off-White (#F5F5F0): Main background color
  • Dark Gray (#2C2C2C): Primary body text
